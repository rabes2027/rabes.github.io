Torn Between Two Hearts

A Shakespearean Sonnet By: Raphael Mark Abes


Oh how the heart can break, and love can turn to dust

As I stand here, and watch you walk astray

The memories we shared, like ashes they must,

Be scattered in the wind, and fade away


The pain I feel, like a thousand whips,

That pierce my soul, and leave me breathless to present time

I try to hold on, only for you to slip from my grips

As sand through fingers, descending like grime


Your smile still haunts me as I get old

But now I see the truth in what you said

That I was never enough for you to hold

And now I'm left with nothing but regret instead


So here I stand, a broken hearted fool

As you disappear in the misty haze of cool
